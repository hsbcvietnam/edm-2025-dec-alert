Stay alert against scam
 
Dear Valued Customer,
We would like to inform you that fraudsters are employing various tactics to scam customers. Please be aware as they can:
Fraudsters send phishing SMS under well-known organizations such as Netflix.   Victims are tricked to share card details and OTP.   Fraudster add cards to Samsung devices and make fraudulent transactions via SamsungPay.
or
Impersonate bank or vendors.   Ask to pay for card delivery or prompt you to click on a link to cancel membership, redeem points, or block card, etc.
You can follow these preventives tips
to keep yourself secured:
Check notification emails from the bank regularly and report to HSBC Contact Centre immediately if your card is provisioned to Samsung devices without your request.
Don't share your online banking passwords, PINs, OTPs, or card details with anyone. This includes bank staff members or anyone who claims to be a vendor of the Bank.
Don't click on links or scan QR codes provided in unverified emails, SMS messages or social media messages. Never input an OTP requested through a link provided in an email or SMS.
Don't install apps from untrusted sources or apps that require high-level permission on your device. Only download HSBC Vietnam app via App store or Google Play store. HSBC Vietnam app does not support any beta or unofficial iOS or Android releases.
Don't rush to act. Fraudsters leverage on urgency to trick you into their scams. Stop and think before making payment transfers or online card transactions.
Be cautious of every request or offer, verify it directly with the organization, including HSBC, via official channels.
Important Points to Remember:
Common card services such as point redemption, card block/unblock can only be performed through official channels like HSBC Mobile Banking App or Contact Centre.
HSBC never asks you to pay any fee related to card delivery services even delivery address adjustment.
You may refer to our official contact channels provided on HSBC's website (https://www.hsbc.com.vn/)
  (**)Social networking sites such as Facebook and WhatsApp are only for the purpose of providing and exchanging information, which must not be used as official transaction channels.
